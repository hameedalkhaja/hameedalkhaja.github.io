Volleyball rules

volleyball has 7 positions they are outside hitter, opposite, setter, libero, defensive specialist, serving specialist and middle blocker

a team may only touch the ball 3 times at a time, if a player has touched the ball the may not touch it until another player has done the same

when serving a player must not cross the outside line

players are only allowed to hit the ball not carry it, players may also hit the ball with any part of their body

a point is scored whenever a team gets the ball to touch the opposing sides floor, or the ball touches the outside of the pitch after it has last been touched by the opposing team

Volleyball Facts

Volleyball only became an olympic sport in 1964, regardless of the fact that it was made in 1895 by William G. Morgan

Proffesional volleyball players jump as many as 300 times on average in a single game, thus leg fitness and strength are crucial to the sport

professional male volleyball players have an average serve speed of 70-80 miles per hour
